About Me

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Physics at the CUNY Graduate Center and Hunter College in New York City, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant. My research interests include quantum information theory, quantum randomness and foundations, event-based and neuromorphic vision, computational optics, machine learning for imaging, and numerical methods for quantum dynamics. I'm also interested in deep learning generalization and training dynamics, including phenomena like grokking.

Before starting my Ph.D., I earned a B.S. in Physics with minors in Mathematics and Political Science from Nebraska Wesleyan University. I also teach as a Graduate Adjunct Lecturer in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at Hunter College, where I lead instructional laboratories for introductory physics courses.
